Why Local Search Matters More Than National for Trades

Here's the thing most tradespeople get wrong about online marketing: you don't need to rank nationally. You need to dominate locally. Here's why local SEO is your secret weapon:

Your Competition is Smaller

Instead of competing with every plumber in the UK, you're only competing with plumbers in your town. Much easier odds.

Higher Intent Customers

Someone searching for "plumber near me" is ready to hire. Someone searching for "plumbing tips" is just browsing.

Google Loves Local

Google wants to show relevant local results. A well-optimized local business can outrank massive national companies.

Mobile-First World

79% of trade searches happen on mobile, usually with "near me" or a location. Be there when they search.

The "Local SEO Sweet Spot" for UK Trades

The perfect local SEO strategy covers:

  • ✓ Your immediate area (2-3 mile radius)
  • ✓ Surrounding towns/villages you'll travel to
  • ✓ Emergency services if you offer them
  • ✓ Specialized services you're known for

Most trades think local SEO is complicated. It's not. Follow our step-by-step guide below and you'll start showing up in local searches within weeks.

Google My Business Mastery for Tradespeople

Your Google My Business (GMB) listing is the most important thing for local SEO. It's free, it's powerful, and most trades set it up wrong. Here's how to do it right:

GMB Setup Checklist for UK Trades

Essential Information:

  • ✓ Exact business name (consistent everywhere)
  • ✓ Complete address (or service area if mobile)
  • ✓ Local phone number (not 0800 numbers)
  • ✓ Website URL (your SmartSiteBot site)
  • ✓ Correct business category

Optimization Essentials:

  • ✓ Professional photos (van, team, work)
  • ✓ Regular posts about your work
  • ✓ Accurate opening hours
  • ✓ Services list (all the work you do)
  • ✓ Description with local keywords

Common GMB Mistakes Costing You Customers

❌ Wrong Business Category

Don't choose "General Contractor" when you're a plumber. Google uses categories to decide when to show your business.

❌ No Photos of Your Work

Customers want to see your van, your team, and examples of your work. Empty GMB listings look suspicious.

❌ Ignoring Customer Questions

Google lets customers ask questions on your listing. Not answering makes you look unprofessional.

Review Generation Strategies That Don't Backfire

Google reviews are crucial for local SEO, but there's a right way and wrong way to get them. Here's what works (and what gets you banned):

⚠️ What NOT to Do (Google Will Ban You)

  • ❌ Buy fake reviews
  • ❌ Ask family/friends to leave reviews
  • ❌ Offer discounts for reviews
  • ❌ Review yourself from different accounts
  • ❌ Ask customers to remove bad reviews

What Actually Works (And Keeps You Safe)

The Follow-Up Text Method

Send this text 2 days after completing work:

"Hi [Name], hope you're happy with the [work done]. If you've got 30 seconds, a quick Google review would really help our small business. Thanks! - [Your name]"

The Thank You Card Method

Leave a card after great work:

"Thanks for choosing us! If you're happy with our work, we'd appreciate a quick review on Google. It really helps other local people find our services."

Remember: only ask happy customers, never pressure anyone, and always provide excellent service first. For more review strategies that actually work, read our guide on getting customer reviews safely.

Common Local SEO Mistakes Costing You Customers

Inconsistent Business Information

Your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) must be identical everywhere online.

✓ Good: "Smith Plumbing Services" everywhere
❌ Bad: "Smith Plumbing" on website, "Smith Plumbing Services Ltd" on GMB

Not Optimizing for "Near Me" Searches

46% of Google searches include location terms. Make sure you're optimized for them.

✓ What to optimize for:
  • • "plumber near me"
  • • "electrician near me"
  • • "emergency plumber near me"
How to optimize:
  • • Include "near you" in content
  • • Add local landmarks
  • • Mention service areas

Ignoring Mobile Experience

79% of local searches happen on mobile. If your site doesn't work perfectly on phones, you're losing customers.
